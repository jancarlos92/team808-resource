The Battle Cards

Positioning, comparison tables, wedge questions, and landmines for the five systems you'll hear most. Study the wedge questions hardest — the goal is never to win an argument, it's to open a door.

The Ground Rule Never trash the competitor. Respect their system, then ask a question their system makes hard to answer. Prospects defend what you attack — and get curious about what you question.

⚔ BATTLE Sage Intacct vs Restaurant365 Use when prospect mentions R365 or hospitality
Use When They Say
"We use Restaurant365" "We're in restaurants / hospitality" "Better reporting across locations" "Evaluating accounting platforms"
⚡ 10-SECOND POSITIONING — SAY THIS "Restaurant365 is strong for restaurant operations and location-level workflows. Sage Intacct usually wins when finance needs scalable multi-entity accounting, faster close, and flexible reporting across properties, outlets, and business lines — especially as complexity grows."
Accounting Area Why It Matters ✅ Sage Intacct ⚠ R365 Reality
Multi-entity / multi-property consolidation Groups expand fast: locations, entities, ownership structures Built for multi-entity consolidation + intercompany clarity Strong for multi-location restaurants, but consolidation depth varies by structure
Dimensional reporting Finance needs P&L by property/outlet/channel without COA chaos Dimensions let you slice results without rebuilding chart of accounts Strong restaurant reporting, but feels more "restaurant model" vs flexible finance dimensions
Close speed + controls at scale Allocations, accruals, approvals get heavier as you grow Strong financial close discipline, role controls, audit trail Finance teams may want more ERP-grade close/controls as complexity rises
Corporate finance visibility Hospitality often includes lodging + F&B + events + mgmt entities Designed for broader entity types and corporate finance reporting Excellent for restaurant-centric orgs; cross-vertical expansion may need more structure
AP automation + spend governance Vendor spend leakage hits margin fast Strong AP workflows, approvals, vendor analytics AP is solid, but depth of configurable workflows varies by use case
Budgeting / forecasting Labor + demand swings require real forecasting Budget-to-actual, scenario planning, finance-led forecasting Strong operational planning for restaurants; finance forecasting maturity varies
Scaling beyond "ops-first" Leaders need finance to drive decisions, not just report history Finance-first system optimized for accounting teams Ops-first platform; finance-led flexibility may be the gap at scale
🔍 Wedge Questions — Ask 1–2, then stop
"How many locations/entities do you manage — and do you consolidate across owners or management companies?"
"Can you get a clean P&L by location + department + channel without exporting to Excel?"
"How long is month-end close — and what's the #1 bottleneck?"
"Do you need financial reporting across multiple business lines (lodging + F&B + events)?"
"How confident are you that vendor spend and approvals are controlled consistently across locations?"
✅ Good Pain Signals — Listen for These
"We're growing and reporting is getting messy."
"We need better consolidated reporting across entities."
"We still pull things into spreadsheets to get leadership views."
"Month-end close keeps slipping as we add locations."
"We can't easily drill down into what's driving margin changes."
"Approvals and controls aren't consistent across locations."
"We need reporting beyond restaurants — more corporate-level finance visibility."
🧠 Talk Track
RESPECT "Restaurant365 is great at restaurant operations and location-level workflows."
WEDGE "Where Intacct tends to win is when finance needs more scalable consolidation, cleaner close processes, and flexible reporting across multiple entity types — especially as you grow into more complexity."
LAND IT "So if your finance team is doing manual work to consolidate, drill down, or build board-ready reporting… that's exactly the gap we help close."
🧨 Landmines — Don't Say These
❌ "R365 can't do that."
✅ Say: "R365 is strong operationally — Intacct is typically stronger for finance-led scalability and consolidation."
❌ "You should rip and replace."
✅ Say: "This is about whether your system matches your next stage of growth."
🏁 CLEAN CTA "If we could show you a multi-entity consolidated view with drill-down by location and department — and reduce spreadsheet reporting while tightening spend control — would it be worth 15 minutes to benchmark your current setup?"
🧩 When R365 Might Be the Better Fit — Be Honest
The business is primarily restaurant-only and wants an ops-first platform
They value restaurant-specific workflows above corporate finance complexity
Finance reporting needs are straightforward and consolidation is light

Sage Intacct typically wins when finance needs modern multi-entity reporting, faster close, and cleaner accounting controls — while still integrating to the operational systems that dispatch and billing teams rely on."
Accounting Area Why It Matters ✅ Sage Intacct ⚠ McLeod Reality
Finance-first reporting (entity/dept/location) Leadership needs fast answers without spreadsheet work Dimensional reporting + drill-down built for finance teams Finance reporting often needs extra work/exporting for executive views
Multi-entity consolidation Logistics groups may have multiple entities, terminals, or business lines Built for multi-entity consolidation, intercompany, and consolidated reporting Complex consolidations may become more manual/process-heavy
Close speed + repeatability Close gets heavier with accruals, allocations, and reconciliations Accounting workflows designed to improve close consistency and control Teams may rely on legacy processes tied to operational workflows and manual close steps
Accounting controls + audit trail Controllers need approval discipline and clean auditability Strong role-based controls, approvals, and finance governance Finance teams may want deeper accounting workflow standardization
Scalable financial planning/reporting CFO/VP Finance need board-ready reporting as company grows Finance-led structure supports cleaner budget vs actual and scalable reporting TMS-first systems can be excellent operationally, but finance reporting flexibility may be the gap
Best-of-breed architecture Ops and finance need different strengths but must stay connected Intacct serves as finance hub while McLeod remains operational system of record Keeping everything in one system feels simpler, but finance depth/flexibility may be the tradeoff
Self-service finance agility Controllers need report changes without heavy technical support Finance users can build/drill reports faster Reporting changes may depend on exports or specialized support knowledge

STEP 1"McLeod is great at running trucking operations — dispatch, loads, and day-to-day execution."

QUALIFY"Are you optimizing for keeping accounting tied to operations, or for giving finance a cleaner, faster reporting and close process while staying integrated?"
WEDGE"What we see often is: logistics teams keep McLeod for operations, but move finance to Intacct when the accounting team needs better visibility and less manual reporting."

⚔ BATTLE Sage Intacct vs M3 Accounting Use when prospect mentions M3 or hotel portfolio consolidation pain
Use When They Say
"We're on M3 / M3 Core" "Corporate reporting is hard" "Better consolidation across entities" "Faster close / less Excel for ownership reporting"
⚡ 10-SECOND POSITIONING — SAY THIS "M3 is purpose-built hotel accounting software with strong integrations and daily hotel workflows. Sage Intacct typically wins when an ownership/management group needs finance-led multi-entity consolidation, dimensional reporting across the portfolio, and stronger corporate controls — while still integrating to hotel systems."
Accounting Area Why It Matters ✅ Sage Intacct ⚠ M3 Reality
Multi-entity + portfolio consolidation Ownership groups need consolidated financials across properties/entities Built for multi-entity setup + consolidation with configurable rules and entity inheritance Strong for multi-property hotel accounting, but consolidation depth can vary by portfolio needs
Dimensional reporting (property/dept/concept/region) P&L views without building a massive chart of accounts Slice by location/group/concept/region and drill down fast Strong hotel-specific views, but finance teams may want broader corporate slice-and-dice flexibility
Speed to ownership/board-ready reporting Owners want answers mid-month, not after close Finance-led reporting + drill-down reduces spreadsheet packaging M3 provides daily snapshots and dashboards — strong operationally, but portfolio packaging can require extra work
Close process + repeatability Portfolio close gets heavy: allocations, intercompany, reconciliations Standardized finance workflows; multi-entity rules reduce manual repeat work Complexity can grow as entity structures and reporting demands expand
Controls + audit trail + role separation Mgmt co. vs ownership vs property staff access needs Strong role-based controls across entities/processes (corporate governance) Controls depend on configuration and how broadly M3 is used across finance functions
Integration strategy (keep hotel systems) Must keep PMS/night audit/POS/payroll connected Fits "finance hub" model integrating with hotel ecosystem M3 highlights seamless hotel integrations — strong for ops, but finance hub flexibility varies
Corporate finance scaling As groups grow, finance needs more entity complexity and governance Multi-entity design supports scaling finance across growing portfolios M3 excels at hotel ops/accounting workflows — may be exactly right for some groups
🎯 Integration-First Attack Plan
VALIDATE"M3 is built for hotels — especially daily hotel accounting and PMS/night audit integration."
SEPARATE"For ownership/management groups, the question becomes: can finance consolidate and report across the portfolio fast enough for decisions — without Excel packaging?"
QUALIFY"Are you optimizing for hotel workflow convenience, or for portfolio-level finance speed and visibility for ownership?"
POSITION"Many groups keep M3 for hotel workflow + add Intacct as the finance reporting/consolidation layer."
🔍 Wedge Questions — Ask 1–2, then stop
"How many legal entities and properties are you consolidating — and how manual is that today?"
"Can you produce a portfolio P&L by region/brand/management vs ownership without Excel?"
"How quickly can ownership get answers mid-month — same day, same week, or after close?"
"Are you satisfied with reporting flexibility when leadership asks for a new cut of the data?"
"Do you want to keep M3 as the hotel system and strengthen corporate finance reporting on top?"

"We export to Excel to build ownership packages."
"Consolidation takes too long across entities."
"Every new reporting request becomes a project."

"We need stronger controls and consistency across properties."
