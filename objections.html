The Objection Library

All 30 objections you'll hear, sorted into the five families they actually belong to. Learn the family and you can handle wordings you've never heard — the response pattern is the same.

The Universal Pattern — Every Response Below Follows It

1 · Acknowledge

Agree first. Resistance melts when there's nothing to push against.

"Totally understand…" / "That's fair…"
2 · Reframe

Shift from their objection to the pain underneath it — usually with context about companies like theirs.

"…most teams we work with said the same thing until…"
3 · Ask

End with one small question. A question keeps the call alive; a statement ends it.

"…can I ask, how much of that is still manual?"

Brush-Offs 6

The reflex, not the real answer. They're rejecting the interruption, not Sage Intacct — they haven't heard enough to reject anything. Your only goal is to earn 30 more seconds, usually by agreeing with them first.

📌 Agree fast, ask small.
"Send me something" ▶

"Happy to — I want to make sure I send something relevant. Quick question: what's the biggest pain in your current reporting or job tracking process?"

"Not interested" ▶

CLARIFY
"Totally fair — is that because it's bad timing, or you're already set with how you handle finance today?"

RESPOND
"Got it. I'm not calling to pitch — I'm trying to see if this is even relevant."

CTA
"If I ask one quick question: how long does month-end close take right now?"

"Just send me info" ▶

CLARIFY
"Happy to — quick question so I send something useful: are you focused more on reporting, close speed, or multi-location complexity?"

RESPOND
"Perfect. I'll send the short version — no fluff. I'll include one example from a similar team."

CTA
"If it's relevant, worth 15 minutes to compare notes next week?"

"We're in the middle of something" ▶

CLARIFY
"Implementation, audit, acquisition, or growth project?"

RESPOND
"Totally fair — I don't want to add noise. Teams often plan the next step while they're mid-project."

CTA
"Should I reconnect right after it wraps, or 2–3 weeks before?"

"I don't take cold calls" ▶

CLARIFY
"Fair — do you prefer email, or should I reach out to someone else?"

RESPOND
"I'll be brief — the reason I called is we see teams hit reporting/close limits as they grow. I'm only trying to confirm whether that's happening there."

CTA
"If I send 3 bullets, would you take a look?"

"Just email me" ▶

CLARIFY
"Perfect — should I anchor it to reporting, close speed, or multi-location visibility?"

RESPOND
"Got it — I'll send the short version. One relevant example, not a deck."

CTA
"If it looks relevant, is 15 minutes next week crazy?"

Status Quo 13

The most common family by far. Comfort with the current system feels like satisfaction, but 'happy' usually means 'used to it.' Never attack their choice — introduce doubt with a question about the workarounds living around their system.

📌 Never fight the system. Ask about the workarounds.
"We're happy with what we have" ▶

"Totally understand — most companies we end up working with said the same thing. Can I ask, when it comes to [job profitability / multi-location reporting / closing the books], how much of that is still manual today?"

"We just implemented something" ▶

"Got it — how long ago? The reason I ask is that a lot of companies hit limitations pretty quickly once complexity or location count picks up."

"We went with a competitor" ▶

"Congrats — who'd you go with? [Pause] How's it going so far? [Listen — circle back in 6–12 months]"

"QuickBooks works fine" ▶

CLARIFY
"How many locations/entities are you managing today?"

RESPOND
"QuickBooks is solid early — it usually strains once reporting and close get more complex. Teams move when consolidated reporting becomes spreadsheet-heavy."

CTA
"Quick benchmark: how manual is reporting today?"

"We use NetSuite" ▶

CLARIFY
"Are you happy with reporting and agility today?"

RESPOND
"Totally makes sense — teams only look elsewhere if something becomes painful. Usually it's admin overhead or reporting friction."

CTA
"If you could improve one thing, what would it be — reporting, close, or admin overhead?"

"We already have a system" ▶

CLARIFY
"What are you using today?"

RESPOND
"Nice — then it's not 'replacement,' it's whether it still fits where you're headed. Growth usually exposes reporting and control gaps."

CTA
"How long does close take — and is it getting better or worse?"

"We're good with what we have" ▶

CLARIFY
"What part works best — close, reporting, or controls?"

RESPOND
"Love hearing that — I'm only reaching out because teams usually feel that way right before growth adds friction."

CTA
"If you grow 25% this year, what breaks first?"

"We don't want to switch systems" ▶

CLARIFY
"Is the concern more risk, effort, or disruption?"

RESPOND
"Totally valid — I wouldn't switch unless it was clearly worth it. There are staged approaches that reduce disruption."

CTA
"If nothing changes, what breaks first in the next 12 months?"

"We're locked into a contract" ▶

CLARIFY
"When does renewal come up?"

RESPOND
"Got it — then this is a planning conversation, not a switch today. Teams often benchmark options 60–90 days before renewal."

CTA
"Want to baseline now so you're not rushed later?"

"We're a small team" ▶

CLARIFY
"How many people touch close and reporting today?"

RESPOND
"Exactly — small teams need systems that remove manual effort. Wins are time back and fewer errors."

CTA
"If you could eliminate one manual process, what would it be?"

"We don't have that problem" ▶

CLARIFY
"Just to sanity check — how long does close take today?"

RESPOND
"Nice — then you're ahead of most teams. Pain usually shows up with growth or new entities."

CTA
"If you grow 25% this year, what breaks first?"

"We evaluated this before" ▶

CLARIFY
"What ruled it out last time — timing, cost, fit, or complexity?"

RESPOND
"Helpful — things may have changed since then. Pain grows as reporting and close complexity increases."

CTA
"Worth a quick revisit if your needs changed?"

"We outsource finance" ▶

CLARIFY
"Do they handle reporting too — or mainly bookkeeping/tax?"

RESPOND
"Got it — the pain usually shows up when leadership needs faster insight. Most teams still need location/entity visibility internally."

CTA
"How quickly can you get location-level performance today?"

Budget 3

Almost always premature — you never mentioned a price, so this is really 'this isn't a priority.' Separate priority from price: the first meeting costs nothing, and knowing what modern looks like is free.

📌 You never quoted a price. Don't defend one.
"We don't have budget right now" ▶

"That makes sense. Most of our conversations aren't about budget right now — they're about understanding whether what you're running is going to scale with you. Is 20 minutes worth figuring that out?"

"No budget" ▶

CLARIFY
"Is it no budget at all, or just not allocated right now?"

RESPOND
"Totally fair. Most teams justify it through time saved at close and less manual reporting."

CTA
"Worth a quick call to see if it would pay for itself?"

"Too expensive" ▶

CLARIFY
"Compared to what — cost today, effort, or risk?"

RESPOND
"Totally fair — the goal is usually to reduce manual reporting and speed close. Most teams justify this by getting time back and fewer errors."

CTA
"If we could reduce close time by 30–50%, worth benchmarking?"

Timing 5

The most honest-sounding objection and the one that quietly kills the most pipeline. 'Later' is where deals go to die — convert vague later into a cheap, dated commitment on the calendar.

📌 Vague later → dated later. Always.
"We're too busy right now" ▶

"I get it — this is a 20-minute call, not a project. What's making it so hectic? [Pause] That's usually exactly what we help with."

"Call me back next quarter" ▶

"Happy to — I'll put it in the calendar. One question before I let you go: what would need to change by then for it to be worth a look?"

"We're too busy" ▶

CLARIFY
"Busy because of close, audit, or growth/fire drills?"

RESPOND
"Totally get it — usually that's when systems start showing cracks."

CTA
"If I keep it to 12 minutes, worth seeing if there's a faster path?"

"Not looking right now" ▶

CLARIFY
"Is it because things are stable, or priorities are elsewhere?"

RESPOND
"Got it — I'm not trying to force timing. Teams start planning when close/reporting starts slowing decisions."

CTA
"What would need to change for it to be worth looking?"

"Talk to me when it's urgent" ▶

CLARIFY
"What makes it urgent — audit issues, close delays, or reporting pressure?"

RESPOND
"Totally fair — urgency is when it hurts. Best planning happens slightly before the breaking point."

CTA
"What would need to happen for this to move up the list?"

Wrong Person 3

Not a rejection — it's a map. They just offered to tell you who the right person is. Thank them, extract the referral, and grab one piece of intel about the finance office before you go.

📌 It's a referral wearing a disguise.
"I'm not the right person" ▶

"No worries — who would be? And while I have you — what system is the finance team using today?"

"Finance handles this" ▶

CLARIFY
"Totally — who in finance owns reporting and close improvements?"

RESPOND
"Perfect, I'll connect with them. This affects close speed, controls, and visibility."

CTA
"Can you point me to the right person — Controller or CFO?"

"Not my department" ▶

CLARIFY
"Who usually owns this — finance leadership or ops finance?"

RESPOND
"Totally fair — I'll contact the right team. This decision is usually driven by reporting and close pain."

CTA
"Who's best — Controller or CFO?"
