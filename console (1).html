BANT Qualification Framework Work B → A → N → T in order. One question per category.
B — BUDGET
⭐ "How is your finance technology budget structured — does that sit with you or is there a separate process for systems like this?"
"When your company has evaluated new systems in the past, what did that process look like?"
"Is there a fiscal year cycle we should be mindful of — when do budgets typically get approved?"
"A lot of companies fund this through operational savings — has your team explored that angle?"
A — AUTHORITY
⭐ "If you saw something that made sense here — what would the decision process look like at your company?"
"Who else on your team would need to weigh in — IT, ownership, the board?"
"Have you evaluated or switched financial systems before? What did that process look like?"
"Is there an owner or executive who's been pushing for better financial visibility?"
N — NEED
⭐ "Where does your current process tend to break down — especially around job profitability or closing the books?"
"How are you tracking profitability at the job, route, or location level today?"
"What does month-end close look like — how much manual work goes into pulling it together?"
"When ownership or leadership asks for a financial snapshot, how long does it take to produce?"
T — TIMELINE
⭐ "Is there a specific event driving this — a new hire, year-end, new entity, audit?"
"If you decided to move forward, when would you ideally want to be live on a new system?"
"Are there any contracts or renewal dates with your current system we should know about?"
"What would need to be true for your team to prioritize this in the next 90 days?"
✅ QUALIFIED → pass to AE: Budget identified + Decision maker engaged + Clear pain + Timeline < 6 months
🟡 DEVELOPING → nurture: 1–2 BANT criteria confirmed. Missing budget clarity or no compelling event.
🔴 DISQUALIFY → move on: No pain, no budget path, no authority access, or 12+ month timeline with no event.
SPIV Discovery Deepeners Use AFTER the prospect is talking. BANT qualifies. SPIV builds urgency.

"If finance had the tools to scale without adding headcount — what does that make possible?"
🏨 DEEP DISC Lodging KPI Discovery Use when prospect is in hotels / hospitality — go deeper than SPIV
The simple story: Hotels don't lose money because they don't track metrics. They lose money because they can't see what's driving the metrics fast enough to act.
RevPAR Revenue per Available Room Rate · Occupancy · Channel Mix ▶
RevPAR tells you how well you're monetizing rooms overall. If RevPAR drops, the business can quietly bleed revenue even if "occupancy looks okay." Helps leadership answer: "Are we underpricing, under-filling, or losing the right guests to the wrong channels?"
Discovery Questions
"When RevPAR dips, can you quickly tell whether it's rate, occupancy, channel mix, or comping/discounting driving it?"
"Do you break RevPAR down by property, room type, day-of-week, and booking source — or is it mostly a single blended number?"
"How long does it take to identify which segment caused a miss and take action — same day, same week, or after month-end?"
"If you had RevPAR by segment with margins, what's the first decision you'd make differently?"
✅ Good Pain Signals
"We don't know if it's rate or occupancy causing the drop."
"We only look at a blended RevPAR — no breakdown by channel or room type."
"We realize the miss after month-end… too late to fix."
"OTAs are filling rooms, but profit isn't improving."
How Intacct Adds Value
Dimensional reporting: RevPAR by property / channel / segment / room class / weekday vs weekend
Finance answers "what's driving the dip" without spreadsheets
Turns RevPAR from a static number into an actionable lever
🏁 CTA"If we could break RevPAR down by channel + room type + day-of-week and show the profit impact, would it be worth 15 minutes to see what decisions you could change immediately?"
⚡ Intacct wedge: dimensional reporting (property / segment / channel / room class) so finance becomes an operator.
ADR Average Daily Rate Pricing · Discounting · Net Revenue ▶
ADR tells you how strong your pricing is — but ADR alone can be misleading because it doesn't show discounting behavior or true profitability after fees. Helps answer: "Are we pricing smart… or just filling rooms at a discount?"
Discovery Questions
"Are you tracking ADR alongside discount reasons and rate overrides — and can you see how that impacts profitability, not just top-line?"
"Where do rate decisions happen, and does finance have a way to validate whether ADR changes are creating real profit improvement?"
"Can you model ADR strategy impacts by channel (direct vs OTA) and compare net ADR after fees?"
⚡ Killer follow-up: "When you look at OTAs, do you track revenue — or do you track net revenue after fees?" (Separates teams that measure activity from teams that measure profit.)
✅ Good Pain Signals
"We're increasing ADR but profit isn't moving."
"Discounting happens and finance finds out later."
"Rate overrides aren't tracked consistently."
"We don't know net ADR after OTA fees."
How Intacct Adds Value
Tracks revenue in a way that supports net revenue visibility (after fees/commissions)
Ties ADR strategy to profit outcomes, not just top-line
Validates whether "higher ADR" is real improvement or artificial
🏁 CTA"If we could show you ADR changes alongside discounting + net revenue after fees, would it be worth a quick call to see where pricing is helping — or quietly hurting margin?"
⚡ Intacct wedge: net revenue visibility and channel-level profitability tracking.
TRevPAR Total Revenue per Available Room F&B · Ancillary · Cross-Dept Attribution ▶
TRevPAR is the "full business" metric — shows whether the property is winning beyond rooms (F&B, parking, events, amenities). Helps answer: "Are guests actually spending once they arrive… or are we leaving money on the table?"
Discovery Questions
"When TRevPAR changes, can you attribute it to rooms vs F&B vs ancillary — and see which stream is carrying the property?"
"Do you have a clean way to connect guest spend behavior to the booking channel or guest profile?"
"How confident are you that cross-department revenue is being attributed correctly across outlets and entities?"
✅ Good Pain Signals
"Rooms are strong but F&B is inconsistent."
"We don't know what revenue stream is carrying the property."
"We can't connect guest spend to booking type (direct vs OTA)."
"Cross-department revenue attribution is messy."
How Intacct Adds Value
Consolidates revenue streams across departments into one clean view
Supports multi-department attribution so numbers tie out without manual work
Enables leadership to see what drives total guest value, not just room revenue
🏁 CTA"If we could show TRevPAR broken into rooms vs F&B vs ancillary and highlight what's driving the gains, would it be worth 15 minutes to map where profitability is actually coming from?"
⚡ Intacct wedge: multi-department attribution + consolidated reporting across revenue streams.
GOPPAR Gross Operating Profit per Available Room Labor · Spend · Vendor Leakage ▶
GOPPAR tells you profit per available room after operating expenses. RevPAR can look good while GOPPAR suffers — meaning you're busy, but costs are out of control.
Discovery Questions
"When GOPPAR falls, can you pinpoint the driver fast — labor, food cost, maintenance, utilities, OTA fees, or procurement variance?"
"Are you measuring GOPPAR by property and also by department/outlet, or does it stop at the property level?"
"Do managers see GOPPAR early enough to react, or is this mostly visible after the month closes?"
"If you could drill from GOPPAR → expense category → vendor → purchase patterns, where do you suspect the biggest leak is?"
✅ Good Pain Signals
"We don't know what expense is killing profit."
"We can't see GOPPAR by department/outlet."
"We find margin issues after the month closes."
"Vendor costs creep and nobody catches it fast."
How Intacct Adds Value
Drill-down profitability: GOPPAR → expense category → vendor → transactions
Multi-entity/location reporting to compare properties side-by-side
Strong AP visibility + controls to reduce spend leakage
Helps managers act mid-month, not "after close"
🏁 CTA"If we could show GOPPAR drivers by labor, utilities, maintenance, and vendor spend — and make it visible early enough to act — would it be worth 15 minutes to see where your profit leaks are hiding?"
⚡ Intacct wedge: drill-down profitability + spend governance (AP + dimensions + vendor analytics).
DIRECT Direct Booking Ratio / Channel CPA OTA Fees · Net Margin · Channel Mix ▶
Direct booking ratio shows how many bookings come straight from guests vs through OTAs. OTAs often take 15–25% in fees — so you can be busy but less profitable. Helps answer: "Are we winning the right bookings — or paying too much to get them?"
Discovery Questions
"What's your current direct booking ratio, and do you know how much margin you give up through OTAs after fees?"
"Do you track direct vs OTA performance by property, season, and campaign — or is that data living in different systems?"
"If you increased direct bookings by 5%, do you know what that would do to profit, not just revenue?"
⚡ Killer question: "When you look at OTAs, do you track revenue… or net revenue after fees?" — separates teams that measure activity from teams that measure profit.
✅ Good Pain Signals
"OTAs are filling rooms, but profit isn't improving."
"We don't know our true margin after fees."
"Marketing says campaigns are working, but finance can't tie it to profit."
"Our data lives in different systems so it's hard to compare apples-to-apples."
"We don't know what a 5% direct booking lift would do to EBITDA."
How Intacct Adds Value
Net revenue reporting: shows what you actually keep after OTA commissions and fees
Channel-level margin transparency: direct vs OTA by property / season / campaign
Helps identify which properties are overly OTA-dependent
Finance can answer "which channel is profitable?" without waiting for month-end
🏁 CTA"If we could show you direct vs OTA performance with net margin after fees — by property and season — and quantify what a 5% lift in direct bookings does to profit, would it be worth a quick 15-minute call?"
⚡ Intacct wedge: net revenue reporting + channel-level margin transparency.
